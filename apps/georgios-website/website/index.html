Some things I've built

  • A home network you can rebuild from scratch / open source
    The repo behind this very page, and the rest of my house. Two dozen self-hosted services, from media and photos to a local LLM and an autonomous AI agent, running on a handful of second-hand mini-PCs and Raspberry Pis, all defined in Ansible and rebuildable from a wiped disk with one reflash. GitHub Actions builds the images, a MikroTik segments everything into firewalled VLANs, and Tailscale ties it together. I open-sourced it so anyone can lift the patterns: github.com/gmourtz/gws_homelab
  • Self-serve AI agents for analysts / Rightmove, ongoing
    Putting AI agents into the data platform so people can get answers themselves instead of queuing for an engineer. It's part of bringing 25 years of analytical data up to date.
  • A migration that couldn't lose a row / Rightmove
    I built the checks that proved a 70TB move from Oracle to BigQuery was actually complete, across 25 years of history. I also got the cost of safeguarding all that data down by about a third.
  • Talent search across a billion profiles / Hg
    I designed the pipelines behind a people-intelligence platform that works through more than a billion LinkedIn profiles, so investment teams can search for talent and find gaps across their portfolio. I handled the personal data carefully and shipped it on AWS with Terraform.
  • A faster way to model new data / Arch Capital
    A metadata-driven setup on Snowflake, using Streams, Tasks and Dynamic Tables, that cut the time to model new data by about 30%.
  • Watching a fleet of solar farms in real time / Geodomi
    End-to-end monitoring for 114 MWp of solar assets, from ingestion through to the dashboards people actually used. The insights helped grow revenue by 12%. This is the startup I left the graduate scheme for.
